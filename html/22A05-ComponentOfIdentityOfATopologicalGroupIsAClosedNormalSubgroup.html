component of identity of a topological group is a closed normal subgroup


Theorem - Let G be a topological group and e its identity element. The connected component of e is a closed normal subgroup of G.

Proof: Let F be the connected component of e. All components of a topological space are closed, so F is closed.

Let a∈F. Since the multiplication and inversion functions in G are continuous, the set a⁢F-1 is also connected, and since e∈a⁢F-1 we must have a⁢F-1⊆F. Hence, for every b∈F we have a⁢b-1∈F, i.e. F is a subgroup of G.

If g is an arbitrary element of G, then g-1⁢F⁢g is a connected subset containing e. Hence g-1⁢F⁢g⊂F for every g∈G, i.e. F is a normal subgroup. □
