\begin{definition}
A \emph{Lie groupoid} is is a category $\grp_L= (G_0,G_1)$ in which every arrow or morphism is invertible, and also such that the following conditions are satisfied:

\begin{enumerate}
\item The space of objects $G_0$ and the space of arrows $G_1$ are both smooth manifolds
\item Both structure maps $s,t:G_1 \longrightarrow G_0$ are smooth
\item All structure maps are submersions: $$ s,t: G_1 \longrightarrow G_0,$$ $$u: G_0  \longrightarrow G_1,$$ 
$$i: G_1 \longrightarrow G_1,$$ and $$m: G_1 \times_{s,t} G_1 \longrightarrow G_1$$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}


{\bf Notes:}
A Lie groupoid can be considered as a generalization of a Lie group, but it does have the additional requirements for the groupoid's structure maps that do not have corresponding conditions in the simpler case of the Lie group structure. Because the object space $G_0$ of a Lie groupoid $\grp_L$ is a smooth manifold, $G_0$ is denoted in this case as $M$.
